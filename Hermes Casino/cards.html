Card Games

  • Spanish 21
    Spanish 21 is a fast-paced variation of blackjack that has become increasingly popular. The game is played on a standard blackjack table. The main difference between Spanish 21 compared to blackjack is the removal of all 10s in the decks. Jacks, Queens, and Kings remain, but all natural 10s are removed from every deck used at the table.

  • Texas Hold'Em
    Texas Hold'em Pocket Aces Edition is a card game that uses a standard 52-card deck. Texas Hold’em Pocket Aces edition is similar to traditional poker except it features head-to-head play against the dealer (players do not compete against each other) and optional bonus wagers. Raise up to eight times with Pocket Aces, or you have the option to check until all community cards are exposed. Large payouts for premium Poker Hands.
  • 3 Card Poker
    Three Card Poker is a game where you can make multiple bets on your hand. Ante, Pair Plus and the new 6 Card Bonus. The Ante Wager plays against the Dealer’s hand. The Pair Plus is optional and wins with a Pair or Better. The 6 Card Bonus uses the Dealer’s Cards and your cards to make the best 5 card hand and wins with three of a kind or better and pays 1000 to 1 for a Royal Flush!
  • Baccarat
    The aim of Baccarat is to assemble a hand with a point value totaling as near to 9 as possible. Aces are worth 1, 10s and picture cards are worth 0 whilst all cards from 2-9 retain their face value. If the value of the hand amounts to a two-digit total, disregard the first digit. The last digit represents the baccarat point value.
  • Pai Go
    Pai Gow Poker is played with a standard 52-card deck plus one joker. After making a bet, each player is dealt four tiles and must make two hands. Split your tiles into 2 hands with 2 tiles per hand. Your best hand is your high hand, and the other is your low hand. Then the dealer flips their tiles and compares your high and low hands to the their hands. If yours are more valuable, you win the bet.
  • Blackjack
    The goal is to get your cards as close to 21 as you can without going over, all while beating out the dealer. Number cards are face value, face cards are 10, and aces are your choice of one or 11.
